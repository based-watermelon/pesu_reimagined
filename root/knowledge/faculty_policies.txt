Faculty policies at PES University are designed to maintain a high academic standard and a research-oriented environment. Key policies include: 
Recruitment and Probation
Qualifications: Most core faculty members are required to hold a Ph.D. from a reputed institution and demonstrate strong research credentials.
Probation Period: New faculty members typically serve a probation period of two years. Confirmation of service depends on satisfactory performance in teaching, research, and general conduct.
Ad-hoc Appointments: For urgent vacancies, faculty may be recruited on a temporary ad-hoc basis for one academic year. 
Teaching and Mentorship
Workload: Faculty are expected to strictly adhere to pre-approved lesson plans and course files.
Mentorship: Each faculty member is assigned as a mentor to a small group of students (approx. 20) to monitor their academic and mental well-being.
Availability: Staff must be available on campus during prescribed working hours and are expected to participate in non-academic campus life, including sports and cultural events. 
Research and Innovation
Publication Targets: Faculty are encouraged to publish regularly in indexed journals (UGC CARE/Scopus/Web of Science).
Intellectual Property: Any patentable invention created using University resources must be disclosed; ownership typically rests with the University.
Incentives: The university provides proactive support and incentives for faculty who secure research grants or high-impact publications. 
Code of Conduct and Ethics
Private Employment: Faculty are strictly prohibited from engaging in private tuitions or any other trade/business without written permission from the Vice-Chancellor.
Integrity: Absolute integrity and devotion to duty are mandatory. Any legal proceedings or moral turpitude can result in immediate disciplinary action.
Leave Policy: Leaves (Casual, Sick, or Earned) must be sanctioned in advance. For planned leaves, faculty must arrange for an alternate colleague to handle their classes and lab sessions. 
Attendance and Punctuality
Biometrics: A biometric system is used to monitor daily attendance and punctuality.
Late Entry: Frequent late arrivals or early departures without written permission are viewed as a breach of discipline.
